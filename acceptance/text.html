Acceptance practice

1

Sit comfortably in a chair with your feet on the floor. You can put your hands on your knees or hips.

2

Close your eyes and take a few long breaths through your nose. Feel your stomach rise and fall as you breathe through it.

3

Pay attention to what you hear around. Note that there are many sounds in the world. When you think about these noises and feel condemnation, evaluation, irritation - let your observations and evaluations take a back seat.

4

Keep the focus out of your breath and while staying in that moment, notice all the thoughts and feelings that you have. You may notice some discomfort in your body, or you may have certain thoughts or feelings about what you need to complete or forget to do today.

5

When such thoughts arise, allow them to dissipate without criticizing or lingering on them. Begin to perceive thoughts only as thoughts, without identifying yourself with them. Just watch every moment without condemnation.

6

Think about a situation that is difficult for you to accept. You may not be able to find a job or a life partner, or there may be some collective reality, such as war.

7

Remind yourself that this is the nature of reality. These painful circumstances arise in our lives, in the lives of our loved ones and in the world.

8

Realize that you may not know all the factors that led to this event.

9

Reconcile with the fact that this has already happened. There is no way you can change the past.

10

Remind yourself: "In order for the goiter to influence this situation as positively as possible, I must acknowledge its existence."

11

You can also repeat or reflect on the following passage:

If something can be done, should we despair?

And if there is nothing you can do, what good will despair do you?
